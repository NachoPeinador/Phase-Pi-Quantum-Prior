\begin{abstract}
In the framework of topological superselection theory based on the modular ring $\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z}$, structured initialization of quantum registers demands asymmetric amplitude modulation to confine probability within the resonant channels $1 \pmod 6$ and $5 \pmod 6$. This work demonstrates that the emergence of the optimal phases ($\phi_1 \approx 0.0105$ rad and $\phi_2 = \pi$ rad) is not an empirical artifact but a rigorous analytic consequence. On the one hand, the phase $\pi$ emanates from the isomorphism between the unit group $(\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$ and the discrete group $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$, acting as a non-commutative Berry holonomy that resolves the chiral anomaly of the substrate and reconciles scalar vacuum regularization $\zeta(0)$ with Shannon binary entropy. On the other hand, the thermodynamic asymmetry $\phi_1$ is derived axiomatically as an infrared fixed point of the Renormalization Group beta function, dictated by the Euler-Kronecker invariant. Validated by macroscopic tensor scaling, the state admits an exact representation as a Matrix Product Density Operator (MPDO) with bond dimension $\chi_{\text{MPDO}} \le 36$. Our results provide a rigorous framework for topological protection against decoherence in many-body systems, with direct implications for the suppression of ergodicity (ETH) and the asymptotic reduction of logical depth (T-count) in arithmetic quantum search algorithms.
